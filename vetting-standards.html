Our Methodology

How We Vet Every Brand

Planet Earth Market exists to help you find products that are actually better for the planet, not just marketed that way. Every brand in the marketplace is chosen and scored against the same sustainability and impact criteria, based on what's publicly documented, not on marketing copy.

How we assess impact

Every product carries an impact scorecard across three categories, each on a 1-5 scale:

  • Environmental: materials, packaging, and any climate-related certifications (e.g. Climate Neutral, GOTS, GREENGUARD Gold) the brand discloses.
  • Supply Chain: how transparent the brand is about sourcing, manufacturing, and third-party verification (e.g. B Corp, Fair Trade Certified, published life-cycle assessments).
  • Social: labor standards, giving-back programs, and community impact the brand documents.

These scores are our own editorial judgment based on what each brand publicly discloses, they are not an independent lab certification, and a high score reflects transparency and credible third-party certification, not a guarantee of a product's full environmental footprint.

Hands-on testing, where we can

Most of the brands in the marketplace are also personally purchased and used by our editorial team before being featured. As the marketplace grows to cover more brands, some listings are added based on close research into a brand's certifications, materials, and public disclosures rather than firsthand use, we'll always be clear about which is which as we build that out. Either way, no brand pays for a favorable review or a better impact score.

Affiliate relationships don't change the rating

Some outbound links on this site are affiliate links (see our Affiliate Disclosure). Whether a link is affiliated has no bearing on which products we choose to feature or how we score them.

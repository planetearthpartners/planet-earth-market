Coyuchi is widely regarded as a leader in sustainable home textiles, particularly in bedding and bath products, and its sustainability claims are among the strongest in the category.

What they do well

  • Certified organic materials: Coyuchi is best known for its use of GOTS-certified organic cotton, ensuring lower environmental impact and safer labor practices throughout the supply chain.
  • High transparency: The brand clearly discloses certifications, sourcing practices, and manufacturing partners, going beyond vague "eco-friendly" language.
  • Circular initiatives: Coyuchi's 2nd Home™ Take Back Program allows customers to return used bedding for resale or recycling, directly addressing textile waste.
  • Climate accountability: The company is a Climate Neutral Certified brand and emphasizes emissions measurement and reduction alongside offsets.
  • Durability over disposability: Products are designed to last, encouraging fewer replacements and long-term use.

Where they fall short

  • Price accessibility: Premium materials and ethical production come with higher prices, which may limit access for some consumers.
  • Limited material diversity: While excellent with organic cotton, there's less innovation using alternative fibers compared to some newer textile startups.

Bottom line

Coyuchi is a great choice for sustainability-focused consumers seeking ethically made, organic, and long-lasting bedding and bath products. Its strong certifications, circular programs, and transparency put it near the top of the sustainable home goods space, especially for shoppers prioritizing textiles with real environmental and social standards.

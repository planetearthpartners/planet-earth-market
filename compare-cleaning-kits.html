Bottom line

Both Blueland and Grove Collaborative are Certified B Corps and Climate Neutral®, and both center their model on refillable, low-toxicity home cleaning. Blueland is the more focused option: a single tablet-based refill system across multi-surface, bathroom, glass, and laundry products, with plant-based, EPA Safer Choice-aligned formulas and reusable bottles. Grove Collaborative is broader, a full household marketplace that includes its own refillable private-label line alongside vetted third-party brands, plus it's plastic neutral, offsetting an equivalent amount of plastic to what it sells each year.

If you want a simple, fully refillable cleaning system with the least packaging waste, Blueland is the tighter pick. If you'd rather shop a wider range of household categories under one roof and don't mind that sustainability performance varies across its third-party brands, Grove Collaborative offers more breadth.
